How to Close a Forum and Prevent Further Student Posts

 

 

STOP: If you are a TA, be sure to check with the teacher before carrying out this procedure. Making changes in the Permissions interface can cause a lot of problems if the procedures are not followed exactly. Under no circumstances, should anything else be changed other than what is described below. The teacher is responsible for all changes made to student permissions in their classes.

To prevent further student contributions to a Forum, you can change the Permissions for the activity to make it "read-only."

Note: Unlike "Restrict Access" settings, this method of closing a forum does not hide the grade for the activity from students, and allows them to read posts made before the forum closed. We do not recommend using the "Restrict Access" feature in the settings page because it totally removes the item from student view and from their viewable grade book.

This process must be performed for EACH discussion manually and cannot be set as a scheduled event to occur at a future date and time. The settings will be in effect immediately upon saving the page.

 

 

 

 

1.  From the Course Home Page, open the specific forum that needs adjustment by clicking on Advanced Forums

- Click on the name of the forum to open. Choose Settings > Permissions

 

2.  Locate the "Advanced Role Override" drop down menu. Select "Student."

 

 

 

3.   

 

- Located at the top of the page, just below the page title (See image attachment to the right).
- The page will automatically refresh to display the student permission options that are available.

 

 

4.   Locate the heading "Activity: Advanced Forum"

 

 

Under "Activity: Advanced Forum" Find the following items (see image attachment to the right), then click the "Prevent" button within that line item:
- Reply to posts
- Start new discussions

 

 

 

5.  Scroll to the bottom of the page, click "Save Changes."

 

 

As mentioned above, students will immediately lose the ability for post or reply to discussions within this forum. This setting applies to all students in the class as it is an activity-wide setting, not a specific student setting.

 

 

 

Note: If your forum is currently restricted via the "Restrict Access" settings on the Forum Settings page, you will need to lift the restriction after making this change.
